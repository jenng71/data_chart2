Primetime Performance: NCAA Women’s Finals Break Viewership Records

NCAA women's basketball viewership has been on the rise. Here's a look at viewership for the last 10 championship finals looking ahead to the 2025 final.

November 17, 2024
By Jennifer Garcia Flores

As the 2025 NCAA Women’s Basketball season kicks off, excitement surrounding the sport is at an all-time high, following a historic surge in viewership from 2023 to 2024. A record 18.87 million viewers tuned in for the South Carolina vs. Iowa championship game—nearly double the audience from the previous year and almost 4 million more than the men's final game.

The spotlight on women’s college basketball continues to grow, with John Jay Women's Basketball Coach Lynda Day noting, "You can’t ignore the impact Caitlin Clark has had, people are finally showing up for women's sport." Star players, like UConn’s Paige Bueckers (the expected first draft pick for the 2025 WNBA draft) and Juju Watkins, are leading the way for what promises to be another exciting season, with fans eager to see how the landscape of women’s basketball continues to evolve.
